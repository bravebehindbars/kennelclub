Massachusetts Institute Kennel Club

MISSION STATEMENT:
TO PROVIDE A POWERFUL TOOL TO HELP AUTISTIC YOUTHS WITH THEIR SOCIALIZATION SKILLS!

At Massachusetts Institute Kennel Club our experts focus on making the ideal matches for our clients and canines.At MIKC we are commited to giving back to our youths who are diagnosed with autism spectrum disorders. This is a cause near and dear to our founding members. For the last two decades, MIKC has committted to donating ten canines per year to families with a child who suffers from (ASD) and to offer our services free of charge to these families. We provide quality services and specialize in specific breeds of puppies who will offer the best results with (ASD). Massachusetts Institute Kennel Club provides in-house veteranian services, groomers, and personal trainers who are knowledgable in the specific breeds we offer. Our goal is to provide every service necessary to ensure a one of a kind experience between you, MIKC, and your dog. Our ultimate goal is to help ease any social anxiety,social skills defecits, and social and emotional communication that impedes youths with ASD. Our team of experts have found great results with different breeds depending on where in the spectrum the child may fall on.

Spectrum

Understanding where your child falls on the spectrum is essential in understanding your child's needs, strengths, weaknesses, abilities and disabilities. We encourage you speaking to experts who have a broad knowledge on the subject to ensure that your child may get the canine that will best serve him or her. In the event that your child rather enjoys being alone most time, you can take comfort that he or she will have a silent companion next to him always, whose sole instinct is to comfort them.

Our Vision Statement

Throughout history, we have witnessed dogs playing a key role in human lives in terms of companinonship but also in working side by side with humans to hunt game, herd sheep, and push sleds. Canines are most happy when they serve their master so what better way to put their unique skills to use and in the process bring betterment to lives of youth's that are struggling with physical and emotional socialization.

Our Slogan--"Let Us Learn Together"

Together we an make a change that will not only help families and their children have a better life, but also spread knowledge and understanding of the brilliance those afflicted with (ASD) can achieve with a powerful tool such as a pet companion. Let us all work together to embrace hope, understanding, and assistance to (ASD).

"Parents should consider their children's sensitivities carefully when choosing a pet to ensure a good match, Dr.Carlisle emphasizes. 'Bringing a dog into any family is a big step, but for families of children with autism, getting a dog should be a decision that's taken vey seriously,' she says.
